Senior engineers from one of the world’s fastest-growing tech talent markets. Recruited for your stack, embedded in your team.

Why Pakistan’s Engineering Talent Pool

Pakistan produces over 25,000 IT and computer science graduates annually from institutions including LUMS, NUST, FAST, and GIKI. The country’s IT export revenue has grown at over 20% year-over-year, exceeding $3 billion in recent years. A growing segment of this talent has experience working with international companies in remote-first environments.

The engineering culture is strong in modern stacks: TypeScript, Python, React, Node.js, Go, and cloud-native architectures. English proficiency is high. Many senior engineers have worked with European and American companies and understand Western engineering norms: code review discipline, async communication, sprint ceremonies, and CI/CD-first workflows.

Unlike some nearshore markets where senior talent is scarce, Pakistan has a deep pipeline of mid-to-senior engineers with 5-10+ years of experience building production systems.

The Cost Advantage

Senior engineering salaries in Pakistan are significantly lower than in Germany, reflecting the local cost of living rather than a difference in capability. A senior backend engineer in Berlin costs EUR 127,000-147,000 in year one when fully loaded. The same caliber of engineer from Pakistan, embedded in your team through our model, costs a fraction of that.

This is not about paying engineers less for equivalent work. It is about accessing a market where compensation expectations align with local economics while engineering output aligns with global standards. How We Recruit and Screen

We do not maintain a bench. When you engage us, we recruit engineers specifically for your needs through our established pipeline:

  • Technical screening. We assess against your actual stack, not generic algorithm puzzles. If you run NestJS and PostgreSQL, we test NestJS and PostgreSQL.
  • Communication assessment. Every candidate goes through an async communication exercise: written technical explanation, PR review simulation, and a live technical discussion. Engineers who cannot communicate clearly across timezones do not pass.
  • Domain matching. Where possible, we match engineers with relevant domain experience. An engineer who has built healthcare integrations ramps faster on your healthcare product.
  • Reference verification. We verify past remote work experience and check references from previous international engagements.

Timezone Overlap with Europe

Pakistan Standard Time (UTC+5) provides 3-4 hours of natural overlap with CET/CEST. The working overlap window is typically 12:00 to 16:00 Central European Time.

This window is used for synchronous collaboration: daily standups, pair programming, architecture discussions, and sprint ceremonies. Code reviews, documentation, and focused implementation happen asynchronously. Read our guide on integrating remote developers for the specific practices that make this overlap productive.

What a Typical Engagement Looks Like

  • Week 1: You share your stack requirements, team structure, and immediate needs. We begin recruiting.
  • Weeks 2-3: We present screened candidates matched to your stack. You interview and select.
  • Week 3-4: Engineer joins your team. Structured onboarding: architecture walkthrough, access provisioned, first ticket scoped.
  • Week 4-6: Engineer is contributing to production. Daily pairing sessions during overlap hours. Full participation in sprint ceremonies.
  • Month 2+: Engineer operates independently. Scale the team incrementally based on results.
